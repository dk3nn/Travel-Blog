Travelogue: Beijing

Overview

The ancient city of Beijing, the current capital of China, was founded in 1045 BCE by the Zhou dynasty. The city is known for it's rich culter and history, being home to some of the most important places within all of China. Beijing also has a rich cullianry history and is known for art as well. In the second largest city in China there is plenty to do and see making it a great location to travel to.

Must See Spots

  1. Forbidden City. Home to many of China's Emperors The Forbidden City is necessary to visit when in Beijing. This palace was built without using any nails or glue and made completely out of wood.
  2. Tiananmen, also known as The Gate of Heavenly Peace. This is the main entrance to The Forbidden City though this spot deserves a visit of its own. Built in 1417 it has long been a national symbol of China.
  3. Temple of Heaven. Built during the early 1400s, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is a great example of traditional Chinese architecture.
  4. The Great Wall of China. One of the most famous and iconic places in the world and is considered by many to be one of the greatest creations in human history.
  5. Summer Palace. With gardens, lakes, and palaces spread out over an area larger than the Forbidden City. The Summer Palace containts some of the most beautiful scenery in all of Beijing.

Visitor Tips

  • Book ahead! Many activities and places you may want to see or visit either do not sell same day tickets or have extremely long wait times.
  • Prepare to wait in line. Beijing is the second largest city in China with a population over 20 million people living there, when you are conisdering the over 300 million tourists visting yearly waiting in lines is inevitable. So be Prepared! Especially at popular locations.
  • Get a VPN beofre entering the country. When you are in China many western apps will be blocked, in order to use these apps to communicate with people back home you will need a VPN.
  • Keep your passport with you. In China, your passport is used for all sorts of things, such as checking in to your hotel to being able to enter museums and attractions, you may even need it to access public transportation. So make sure you always have it with you.
